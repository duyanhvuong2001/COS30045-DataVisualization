Sustainability: Energy


As technology and humanity approaches the 21st Century, more and more emphasis is put on the usage of energy in everyday lives.
As such, there are many ways to generate energy, whether if its in an environmentally friendly way or not. The question that we
should have in an energy-dependent world is, "How much of our energy is generated without harming the environment?"



This website seeks to educate and provide information on how much of our energy used is clean energy,
along with some statistics on how much the distribution of energy generation has changed per year in Australia.

Visualization 1: Australia's Distribution Energy, and Clean Energy Generation, 2021


The pie charts presented here visualizes Australia's Overall Distribution of Energy by Type, along with the Clean Energy Distribution in the year 2021. Each pie chart has a legend, which shows the colors used in each pie chart corresponding to a type of energy, and also has a tooltip function to easier see which type of energy is being presented just from hovering the mouse over the graph.


Notice that there are polylines indicating harder-to-see statistics on the graph due to them being too small for mouse-hover or for easy viewing. In addition, in Figure 1, the color green is used to represent the "Renewable" part of the pie chart, in order to highlight this compared to all other non-renewable energy.

Visualization 2: Australia's Dependency on Non-Renewable Fuels in Electricity Generation by states, 2021


This visualization is a combination of a choropleth map, with an interactive bar chart to present how much Australia depends on Non-Renewable Fuels for electricity generation in the year 2021. The choropleth map is divided into states, which on mouse-hover, changes the bar chart to show the amount of electricity generated using specific types of fuels. A legend is used for the choropleth to indicate how much of the total fuels used is non-renewable, with more green indicating the state using more renewable fuels for generation.


Notice that on mouse hover, the choropleth region is then highlighted for easier viewing, with the addition of the color choice in the bar chart, with yellow-brown for non-renewable fuels, while green being renewable fuels.

Visualization 3: Australian States' Shift towards greener energy source, 2010-2021


This visualization is an area chart to present data on the trend of Australian States' shift towards using greener energy sources between the year 2010 to 2021. The graph has an array of buttons that allow for cycling between states, and pressing them changes the area chart below to match the data of the selected state.


Notice that on mouse hover, the specific type of energy source area on the chart will be highlighted for easier viewing of the proportional change of said data. In addition, keeping with the choropleth's coloring scheme, the green color is also used to show renewable energy sources, using different hues/saturations of green to show different data types.
